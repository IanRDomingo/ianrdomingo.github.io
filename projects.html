Applied Scientist Intern Project: Security Classification Architecture
Amazon - Next-Gen Security Automation (SDO) - 2025

Designed and implemented a machine learning system to restructure and improve large-scale security data classification architectures. Developed intelligent schema restructuring mechanisms to map noisy, heterogeneous security signals into a cleaner and more expressive taxonomy. Trained and evaluated classifiers on the restructured architecture, substantially improving classification performance, coverage, and downstream usability of security data.

Checkless: Restaurant Analytics and Inventory Platform
Independent Startup Project - 2024-Present

Co-founded and built a cloud-based restaurant analytics and inventory platform. Designed backend systems using Supabase and PostgreSQL, with frontend deployment via Vercel. Implemented data pipelines and analytics components to model restaurant operations and historical usage patterns, and led system architecture and database schema design.

LLM Memorization and Deterministic Regurgitation
University of California, Irvine - 2024-2025

Investigated memorization behavior in large language models under deterministic decoding regimes. Designed experiments demonstrating that autoregressive self-feeding of model-generated tokens enables verbatim regurgitation of training data, revealing memorization vulnerabilities not captured by standard evaluation methods. This work was accepted at L2M2 @ ACL 2025.

3D Reconstruction and Multi-View Geometry
iMetalX - 2024-2025

Implemented structure-from-motion and multi-view reconstruction pipelines using OpenCV, OpenMVG, and OpenMVS. Reconstructed object-scale and scene-scale geometry from real-world image collections and evaluated reconstruction quality under noisy data and limited camera coverage in an industrial setting.

Neurodegeneration Modeling Project
University of California, Irvine - 2024

Developed machine learning models related to neurodegenerative disease analysis, focusing on representation learning and predictive modeling using biomedical data. Explored model behavior, feature sensitivity, and evaluation considerations relevant to neurodegeneration research. This work was submitted to Nature in 2024.

Generative Modeling for Medical Imaging
University of California, Irvine - 2024

Worked on generative modeling approaches for medical imaging, including variational autoencoders and diffusion-based models. Explored representation learning and reconstruction behavior for medical image slices in a course and research setting, using quantitative metrics to evaluate reconstruction quality and distributional similarity.

Medical Report Generation from Gene and Mutation Data
University of California, Irvine - 2024

Built models that take structured gene and mutation lists as input and generate natural-language diagnostic summaries. Designed preprocessing pipelines to extract biologically relevant features from reports and structured files, and trained supervised models to produce clinically meaningful text outputs.

Multimodal Learning: Text, Speech, and Vision
University of California, Irvine and Purdue University (Collaboration) - 2024

Developed multimodal learning models integrating text, speech, and vision inputs for supervised learning tasks. Implemented cross-attention mechanisms and multimodal fusion strategies to align heterogeneous representations while preserving shared semantic labels. Conducted empirical evaluations addressing modality imbalance, representation alignment, and robustness across modalities.

Neural Balancing for Deep Neural Networks
University of California, Irvine - Intelligent Systems Group - 2023-Present

Developed biologically inspired neural balancing algorithms that balance input and output weight norms to improve training stability and optimization behavior in deep neural networks. Studied balancing in fully connected architectures and extended the framework across multiple model families. Designed controlled experimental pipelines with reproducible training protocols and extensive ablation studies. This work was accepted at NeurIPS 2024 workshops (OPT, SciForDL, and MLNCP), AAAI 2025, and the AI Journal (IJCAI Journal Track, 2025). This line of work forms the core of my master's thesis.

Convolutional Neural Balancing
University of California, Irvine - Intelligent Systems Group - 2023-Present

Extended neural balancing methods to convolutional neural networks, where neuron-level input and output structure is not explicit. Implemented position-wise neuronal balancing of convolutional kernels and compared it against channel balancing and baseline training. Conducted systematic experiments on CIFAR-10 and TinyImageNet using VGG-style architectures, analyzing convergence behavior, optimization stability, and weight norm dynamics. Portions of this work appear in the same venues as the broader neural balancing project.

Sepsis Mortality Prediction
University of California, Irvine and University of Southern California (Collaboration) - 2023-2024

Developed supervised machine learning models to predict patient mortality in sepsis using structured clinical data. Focused on temporal feature extraction from electronic medical records, handling severe class imbalance, and evaluating models using clinically relevant performance metrics. Emphasized robustness and interpretability for clinical decision support. This work was accepted at BMC in 2024.

Tourbillon: Biologically Plausible Neural Architecture
University of California, Irvine - 2023-2024

Worked on the Tourbillon architecture, a biologically inspired neural network model emphasizing local learning dynamics, structured connectivity, and stability properties aligned with biological constraints. Contributed to model implementation, experimentation, and empirical analysis of learning behavior relative to standard deep learning architectures. This work was accepted at UniReps (NeurIPS 2024) and submitted to NLDL 2025.

Database-Driven Recommendation Systems
University of California, Irvine - 2023-2024

Developed recommender system prototypes using relational databases to model user preferences, historical behavior, and contextual signals. Applied these systems to domains such as restaurant recommendations and operational decision support, emphasizing schema design and SQL-driven feature extraction.
